    SEIKO Holdings Corporation

    Japanese maker of watches, clocks, electronic devices, semiconductors, jewelries, and optical products!
    Founder: Kintarō Hattori
    Founded: 1881, Chuo City, Tokyo, Japan
    Headquarters: Minato City, Tokyo, Japan

    History

    The story of Seiko began in 1881, when a 21 year old entrepreneur,Kintaro Hattori, opened a shop selling and repairing watches and clocks in central Tokyo.Today, after more than 130 years of innovation, Kintaro Hattori’s company is still dedicated to the perfection that the founder always strove to achieve. On the following pages you can explore Seiko’s long history.and see many of our landmark watches.

    Recent development

    In 1969, Seiko introduced the Astron, the world's first production quartz watch; when it was introduced, it cost the same as a medium-sized car. Seiko later went on to introduce the first quartz chronograph.
    In the late 1980s, Seiko produced the first automatic quartz that combined the self-energizing attributes of an automatic watch with quartz accuracy. The watch is entirely powered by its movement in everyday wear.[14] In 1991, to increase popularity, these watches were relaunched under the name Seiko Kinetic.[15]
    In 1985, Orient and Seiko established a joint factory.
    Currently watch movements are made in Shizukuishi, Iwate (SII Morioka Seiko Instruments), Ninohe, Iwate (SII Ninohe Tokei Kogyo), Shiojiri, Nagano (Seiko Epson) and their subsidiaries in China, Malaysia and Singapore. The fully integrated in-house production system is still practiced for luxury watches in Japan.

    Marketing

    On Friday, January 10, 2014 on the eve of the Australian Open in Melbourne, Shinji Hattori, President of Seiko Watch Corporation, presented to Novak Djokovic a Seiko 5 limited edition worth $1700. It was launched worldwide with a million units, symbolizing Seiko's partnership with the world's no.1 professional tennis player.

    Presage “Style60’s” Series White Ref. SARY193

    Move to the dressier look, Presage “Style60’s” Series White Ref. SARY193 is here for your everyday life. Among other Seiko automatic watches for men, this Presage truly represents the elegance of a sporty timepiece. It can even be categorized as the best Seiko watches under $500.

    Prospex PADI Turtle 200M Automatic Ref. SBDY093

    A Japanese watch Seiko has collaborated with PADI (Professional Association of Diving Instructors) under the Prospex line since 2016. Its latest model of PADI for 2021, Prospex PADI Turtle 200M Automatic - Special Edition Ref. SBDY093, supposed to be one of the best Seiko watches for men of all time.

    Prospex LX Landmaster Ref. SBDB029

    Seiko widened their collection by creating the Prospex LX in 2019. By blending the 60s professional divers and 90s tool watches, Prospex LX Landmaster Ref. SBDB029 is a result of a true Seiko based on today’s modern taste.

    Presage Chronograph 55th Anniversary Ref. SARK015

    Remembering the achievement of the first Seiko chronograph watch that was crafted in 1954, the brand then released Presage Chronograph 55th Anniversary Ref. SARK015 with high-end movement automatic caliber, 8R48. It vibrates at 28,800 beats per hour and has a 45 hours power reserve with hacking and hand-winding capabilities.

    Presage Automatic Sharp Edged “Akatsuki” 140th Anniversary Ref. SARX085

    Another limited edition Seiko wristwatch you must own is Presage Automatic Sharp Edged “Akatsuki” 140th Anniversary Ref. SARX085. The meaning of dawn in the name of “Akatsuki” truly interprets the entire timepiece colour scheme.

    Prospex 1975 Professional Diver 1000m Tuna 55th Anniversary Ref. SBDX03

    Beside presents you a greater corrosion resistance, Prospex 1975 Professional Diver 1000m Tuna 55th Anniversary Ref. SBDX035 is here with an unexpected 1000 water resistance in a screw-down crown at 4 o’clock position.

    Prospex Alpinist Cream Gilt Ref. SBDC09

    The Alpinist was first introduced in 1961 taking inspiration from Yama-Otoko. Since Japan is mostly covered with mountains, many people do activities in the mountains during their spare time. So, many tools, including wristwatches, are required to fulfill the needs of mountain climbers and sport enthusiasts. Prospex Alpinist is one of the results.

    Prospex Street Series "Urban Safari" Khaki Ref. SBDY059

    The last of the best Seiko watches you must own is Prospex Street Series "Urban Safari" Khaki Ref. SBDY059. This is the first mechanical timepiece for the Seiko’s Street series that drew an adventure spirit in a unique appearance, matching an active person like you. The watch is built in an approachable size of 43.2mm in diameter and height of only 12.7mm.
